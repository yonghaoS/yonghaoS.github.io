Overview Research Publications Under Review Talks Experience

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Simon Fraser University. My research explores how everyday materials, especially wood panels and furniture, can become sensing and actuation interfaces for smart environments.

My work combines human-computer interaction, fabrication, electronics, computational materials, and data-driven system design. Before joining SFU, I worked as a software engineer and project manager at the Institute of Information Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences, and completed degrees in computer science, data science, and electrical and electronic engineering in the United Kingdom and China.

Research

Focus Areas: Human-Computer Interaction, sustainable computational materials, smart environments, fabrication, sensing systems, recyclable electronics, and applied AI.

In my current research agenda, Sustainable Computational Wood, I study how to preserve the natural properties of wood while embedding sensing functions directly into the material. My goal is to make smart furniture and room-scale structures usable from the moment they are built, while keeping ecological impact and recyclability central to the design process.

Recyclable Sensing Materials

I develop conductive and biodegradable composites, carbon-based sensing layers, and passive sensing structures for wood-based computational materials.

Scalable Smart Wood Systems

I design low-cost sensing modules and wireless techniques such as RFID, frequency selective surfaces, and backscatter communication for everyday wooden artifacts.

Real-World Evaluation

I work with woodworkers and users to test usability, sensing reliability, durability, decomposition, and recyclability in furniture and room-scale prototypes.
